Control Valve Failure (Nitrogen Service)

Control valve failure occurs when an automatic control valve on a gas supply line fails in the open position or when a bypass valve is inadvertently opened. This allows maximum gas flow from a high-pressure source into the protected vessel, potentially causing overpressure.

Common scenarios include nitrogen blanket systems, instrument air supplies, inert gas padding systems, and pressure control valves on gas feeds. The failure can result from instrument air loss, controller malfunction, signal failure, or valve actuator failure.

API-521 Section 4.4.8: Failure of Automatic Controls

This case implements the methodology from API-521 Section 4.4.8 for inlet control device failures. Key considerations include:

  • Control Valve Failure: Assumes the valve fails fully open (or is sized at nameplate capacity)
  • Bypass Valve Scenario: API-521 requires considering if bypass valves could be inadvertently opened during operations or maintenance
  • Outlet Flow Credit: Per Section 4.4.8.3, relief rate = maximum inlet flow minus normal outlet flow. Credit can be taken for outlet valves that remain in their normal operating position
  • Relieving Conditions: Flow calculated at maximum upstream supply pressure and vessel relieving pressure (typically 110% MAWP)

Flow Calculation Method: ISA-S75.01 Gas Flow Formulas

The calculations use ISA gas flow equations (referenced in API-520 Part I) which account for choked and non-choked flow conditions through control valves. The formulas require valve flow coefficient (Cv), pressures, temperature, gas molecular weight, specific gravity, and compressibility factor.

Note: This tool is for gas service only. Liquid control valve failures with potential vapor breakthrough require specialized dynamic analysis.

API-521 Section 4.4.8.3: Bypass Valve Consideration

API-521 requires considering if bypass valves could be inadvertently opened during operations or maintenance.

Scenario: Both control valve AND bypass valve are wide open simultaneously.

Calculation: Total Effective Cv = Control Valve Cv + Bypass Valve Cv

API-521 Section 4.4.8.3: Outlet Flow Credit

Per API-521, the required relieving rate is the difference between maximum inlet flow and normal outlet flow.

Credit can be taken for outlet valves that remain in their normal operating position during the control valve failure.

Calculation: Net Relief Flow = Gross Inlet Flow - Normal Outlet Flow
